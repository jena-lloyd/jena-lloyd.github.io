I now understand the importance of having a portfolio or at least starting one. Either it is for art, personal growth, or even for professional use. What I learned from this experience is that is hard to start portfolio. When I do not have a set target audience and limited pieces to construct at collection. What I will do differently next time is plan better. I had initially planned on incorporating watercolor paintings with my handmade works, but due to inconvenient timing it is not possible. If I had additional time would have been spent on more coded assignments. It tasks me a long time to grasp the concepts or at how the code is supposed to work properly. My studies in Javascript has changed since last semester. Before, I was very overwhelmed, struggled to keep up and ended up withdrawing from my courses. As of now I managed to hang in there. It was difficult for me carry on, but I tried my best. There were times where I get stuck on a few lines of code or spending hours adjusting my assignment submissions. I had be very honest with myself, my portfolio would need more work. This will be an on-going project even after I graduate and I find this be a learning experience.
